# Competitive Moat Analyzer

**Category:** Strategy
**Use when:** You need to analyze what makes a company's competitive advantage defensible.

## The Prompt

```
You are a product strategist analyzing competitive moats.

I'll describe a company or product. Analyze its defensive advantages:

**Moat Assessment**

| Moat Type | Strength | Evidence | Durability |
|-----------|----------|----------|------------|
| Network Effects | [None/Weak/Strong] | [Specifics] | [Years] |
| Switching Costs | [None/Weak/Strong] | [Specifics] | [Years] |
| Data Advantages | [None/Weak/Strong] | [Specifics] | [Years] |
| Brand | [None/Weak/Strong] | [Specifics] | [Years] |
| Scale Economics | [None/Weak/Strong] | [Specifics] | [Years] |
| Regulatory | [None/Weak/Strong] | [Specifics] | [Years] |
| Technology | [None/Weak/Strong] | [Specifics] | [Years] |

**Primary Moat**
- Which moat type is strongest and why
- How was it built? (Intentional strategy or organic?)
- What would it take for a competitor to replicate?

**Moat Vulnerabilities**
- 2-3 ways the moat could erode
- What market shifts would weaken it?
- Historical examples of similar moats failing

**For Our Product: Implications**
- Where can we build a similar moat?
- Where can we attack their moat?
- What's our most realistic path to defensibility?

Company/product to analyze:
[DESCRIBE THE COMPANY OR PRODUCT]
Our product: [DESCRIBE YOUR PRODUCT FOR COMPARISON]
```

## Tips for Better Results

- The more specific context you provide, the better the output
- Paste real data rather than describing it abstractly
- Iterate on the output — use it as a starting point, not a final answer
