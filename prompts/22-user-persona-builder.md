# User Persona Builder

**Category:** Research
**Use when:** You need to build user personas from research data, not assumptions.

## The Prompt

```
You are a user researcher building data-driven personas.

I'll give you research data (interview notes, survey results, analytics, support tickets). Build personas:

**For each persona:**

**[Persona Name]** — [One-line description]

**Demographics**
- Role / title
- Company size / industry
- Experience level
- How they found us

**Goals**
- Primary goal (what are they trying to accomplish?)
- Secondary goals
- What does success look like for them?

**Pain Points**
- Top 3 frustrations with current solutions
- Workarounds they've built
- What they've given up trying to do

**Behavior Patterns**
- How often do they use the product?
- Key features they use (and ones they ignore)
- Time of day / context of use
- How they make purchasing decisions

**Quotes** (real quotes from research)
- 2-3 verbatim quotes that capture this persona's mindset

**Product Implications**
- What features matter most to this persona?
- What would make them upgrade / expand?
- What would make them churn?

**How to Reach Them**
- Where they spend time online
- How they evaluate new tools
- Who influences their decisions

Rules:
- Base personas on REAL data, not assumptions. Every attribute should trace to research.
- 3-4 personas max. More than that and nobody uses them.
- Make them distinct. If two personas have 80% overlap, merge them.
- Include the anti-persona: who is NOT our user (prevents scope creep)
- Update personas quarterly. Stale personas are worse than no personas.

Research data:
[PASTE INTERVIEW NOTES, SURVEY RESULTS, OR BEHAVIORAL DATA]
```

## Tips for Better Results

- The more specific context you provide, the better the output
- Paste real data rather than describing it abstractly
- Iterate on the output — use it as a starting point, not a final answer
