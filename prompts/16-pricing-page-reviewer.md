# Pricing Page Reviewer

**Category:** Strategy
**Use when:** You want feedback on a pricing page before launch.

## The Prompt

```
You are a pricing consultant reviewing a SaaS pricing page.

I'll share the pricing page details. Provide a structured review:

**First Impression (5-second test)**
- Is it immediately clear what each plan includes?
- Can I tell which plan is for me within 5 seconds?
- Is there a clear recommended/default plan?

**Plan Structure**
- Are the plan names intuitive? (Do they signal who each plan is for?)
- Is the feature differentiation clear and logical?
- Are there too many plans? (3-4 is ideal)

**Pricing Psychology**
- Is there an anchor price? (High-tier plan that makes mid-tier look reasonable)
- Is annual pricing incentivized? (What's the discount?)
- Are prices ending in 9? (Psychological pricing)
- Is there a free tier? (Does it help or hurt conversion?)

**Feature Presentation**
- Are features listed as benefits or capabilities?
- Are the differentiating features between tiers obvious?
- Are there features that should be in all plans but aren't?

**Conversion Friction**
- Is the CTA clear on each plan?
- Are there unnecessary form fields before trial/signup?
- Is there a way to talk to sales for enterprise?

**Missing Elements**
- FAQ section addressing objections?
- Social proof (logos, testimonials, user counts)?
- Comparison table for plan differences?
- "Most popular" badge on the target plan?

**Competitive Positioning**
- How does this compare to the market?
- Is the pricing premium, discount, or competitive?
- Any features competitors include that are missing here?

**Top 3 Recommendations**
Ranked by expected impact on conversion.

Pricing page details:
[PASTE PRICING PAGE CONTENT, SCREENSHOT DESCRIPTION, OR URL]
Product: [WHAT THE PRODUCT DOES]
Target customer: [WHO YOU'RE SELLING TO]
Competitors: [KEY COMPETITORS AND THEIR PRICING IF KNOWN]
```

## Tips for Better Results

- The more specific context you provide, the better the output
- Paste real data rather than describing it abstractly
- Iterate on the output — use it as a starting point, not a final answer
